R BL AIR FAIL Warning

Understanding and responding to R BL AIR FAIL warnings in the King Air B200

Warning Description
Annunciator
R BL AIR FAIL
Condition
Possible rupture of a bleed air line aft of the firewall shutoff valve, or a leak in the cabin pressurization ducting on the right side
Trigger Point
Temperature reaches approximately 204°F, melting the plastic tubing of the bleed-air warning system
Emergency Checklist
Bleed Air Valve (right engine)
INST & ENVIR OFF
Engine Instruments
MONITOR
R BL AIR OFF Annunciator

Bleed Air System Components
Bleed Air Warning System

Installed to alert the pilot when a pressurization line or pneumatic line ruptures, exhausting hot engine bleed air into the airframe. Plastic tubing lies alongside the insulated pressurization air lines and the uninsulated pneumatic lines.

Excessive heat from a ruptured bleed-air line causes the plastic tubing to fail and could damage surrounding systems, or weaken the structure.

Pressure Switch

Located underneath the floor below the copilot's feet. When the plastic tubing fails and releases pressure, this switch closes, illuminating the applicable BL AIR FAIL light.

NOTE: The bleed-air warning annunciator will not extinguish after closing the bleed-air valves. When the bleed-air control switch is in the OPEN position, it requires DC power to open the flow control unit shutoff valve. When the switch is in the INST & ENVIR OFF position, it requires DC power to close the pneumatic instrument air valve.
BLEED AIR VALVE Switch

Located in the ENVIRONMENTAL group on the copilot's left subpanel. Positions are OPEN, ENVIR OFF, and INST & ENVIR OFF.

  • OPEN: Both environmental flow control unit and pneumatic instrument air valve open
  • ENVIR OFF: Environmental flow control unit closes, pneumatic instrument air valve remains open
  • INST & ENVIR OFF: Both environmental and pneumatic flow valves are closed
System Impact
Engine Performance

A severe bleed-air leak could result in a decrease in engine torque and an increase in ITT. Therefore, whenever the applicable BLEED AIR VALVE switch is placed into INST and ENVIR OFF position, the pilot should monitor the engine instruments for an increase in torque and a decrease in ITT. This indicates that the leak has been isolated, if it was a severe leak.

Pressurization Impact

With one bleed air system inoperative, maximum cabin altitude may be limited. The system uses bleed air from each engine to pressurize the cabin, with flow control units regulating the mixture of engine bleed air and ambient air.

FLIGHT CONSIDERATION: Consider descending to a lower altitude if pressurization cannot be maintained with one bleed air system. Be prepared for possible loss of cabin heat/air conditioning.
Additional Considerations
Required Action

Regardless of engine instruments, any time the bleed-air warning light illuminates, the respective bleed-air valve must be positioned in the INSTrunent and ENVIRonmental OFF position.

Pneumatic System Functions

The pneumatic system supplies bleed air to multiple systems:

  • Vacuum system (via venturi/ejector)
  • Flight hourmeter
  • Brake deice system (hot brakes)
  • Door seal system
  • Surface deice system
  • Rudder boost system
  • Hydraulic reservoir (on certain serial numbers)
