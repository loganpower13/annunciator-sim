Pressurization System Components
Pressurization Controller

Mounted in the pedestal, commands modulation of the outflow valve. Controls cabin pressure altitude and rate of change (200-2,000 fpm).

Dual-scale indicator shows cabin altitude (outer) and maximum aircraft altitude (inner).

Cabin Altimeter

Located on right side panel above throttle quadrant. Continuously monitors actual cabin pressure altitude (outer scale) and cabin differential pressure (inner scale).

CABIN CLIMB Indicator

Located to the left of cabin altimeter. Monitors rate of cabin climb and descent in feet per minute.

ALT WARN Pressure Switch

Located on forward pressure bulkhead. Closes when cabin pressure altitude reaches 12,500 feet, illuminating red ALT WARN annunciator.

NOTE: If auto deployment oxygen system is installed, a separate pressure-sensing switch in cabin wall deploys passenger oxygen masks automatically.
System Limitations
B200 Pressure Differential

Normal working pressure differential: 6.5 ±0.1 psi

Provides cabin altitudes of approximately:

  • 2,800 feet at 20,000 feet aircraft altitude
  • 8,600 feet at 31,000 feet aircraft altitude
  • 10,400 feet at 35,000 feet aircraft altitude
Oxygen System Considerations
WARNING: Adequate oxygen pressure is not provided to passengers for sustained flight at cabin altitudes above 34,000 feet. The highest recommended cabin altitude for sustained flight is 25,000 feet.
