System Information

System Overview

The King Air B200 has two inverters that provide AC power for avionics equipment and engine instruments. Each inverter supplies both 115-volt and 26-volt, 400-Hertz power.

Components Involved

  • Two Inverters: Located in wing center section outboard of each nacelle
  • INVERTER Select Switch: On pilot's left subpanel controls inverter selection
  • Volt-Frequency Meter: Monitors inverter output (105-120 VAC, 380-420 Hz)
  • Inverter Power Relay: Activates when inverter is selected
OPERATIONAL REQUIREMENT: Due to avionics equipment requirements, the 115-volt inverter output must be maintained at 105-120 VAC, 380-420 Hz.
Normal Operation

• Turn the INVERTER select switch to either inverter position

• Verify the INVERTER warning light extinguishes

• Monitor the VOLT-FREQ meter for proper output

• Only one inverter operates at a time

• Inverter operation is controlled by the INVERTER select switch on pilot's left subpanel

Additional Notes

• The red INVERTER warning light illuminates when the active inverter fails

• Selection of the alternate inverter should immediately resolve the issue

• Both inverters are identical and either can power all AC systems

• Inverters are powered from the generator buses through current limiters

• Monitor electrical loads when operating on single inverter

Circuit Protection

INVERTER CONTROL Circuit Breakers:

  • No. 1 INVERTER CONTROL - 5 AMP (Right Side Panel)
  • No. 2 INVERTER CONTROL - 5 AMP (Right Side Panel)

These protect the inverter control switches and control relays.
