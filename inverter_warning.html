AC Power System Components
Inverters

Two inverters installed in wing center section outboard of each nacelle. Each inverter provides both 115-volt and 26-volt, 400-Hertz power for avionics equipment and engine instruments.

Only one inverter operates at a time, selected by the INVERTER select switch on pilot's left subpanel.

INVERTER Select Switch

Located on pilot's left subpanel. Controls which inverter is active. Selection of either inverter activates the inverter power relay and supplies inverter input power.

VOLT-FREQ Meter

Monitors inverter output. Normally reads frequency but will display volts when the button is depressed.

OPERATIONAL REQUIREMENT: Due to avionics equipment requirements, the 115-volt inverter output must be maintained at 105-120 VAC, 380-420 Hz.
Inverter Control Circuit

Inverter operation is controlled by an INVERTER select switch on the pilot's left subpanel. Selection of either inverter activates the inverter power relay and supplies inverter input power.

The inverters are powered from the generator buses through current limiters.

System Limitations
Inverter Output Requirements

Due to avionics equipment requirements, the 115-volt inverter output must be:

  • 105-120 VAC
  • 380-420 Hz
Electrical Load Considerations
NOTE: Monitor electrical loads when operating on single inverter. Both inverters are identical and either can power all AC systems.

Additional Notes
System Redundancy

• The red INVERTER warning light illuminates when the active inverter fails

• Selection of the alternate inverter should immediately resolve the issue

• Both inverters are identical and either can power all AC systems

• Inverters are powered from the generator buses through current limiters

• Monitor electrical loads when operating on single inverter
