L FUEL PRESS Warning

Understanding and responding to L FUEL PRESS warnings in the King Air B200

Warning Description
Annunciator
L FUEL PRESS
Condition
Low fuel pressure detected in the left engine fuel system
Trigger Point
Pressure decreases below 10 ±1 psi
Emergency Checklist
Standby Pump (failed side)
ON
L FUEL PRESS Annunciator
EXTINGUISHED
Fuel System Components
Engine-Driven Boost Pump

Located on the aft accessory section of the engine, this pump delivers low-pressure fuel (25-30 psi) to the high-pressure fuel pump to prevent cavitation.

Operating capacity: 1,250 pph at 25-30 psi.

Standby Boost Pump

Electrically-driven, low-pressure pump located in the bottom of each nacelle tank. Used as backup for engine-driven pump failure.

CAUTION: Engine operation with the fuel pressure annunciator illuminated is limited to 10 hours before overhaul or replacement of the high-pressure main engine fuel pump.
Fuel Pressure Sensing Switch

Located on top of the firewall fuel filter. Closes when pressure decreases below 10±1 psi, illuminating the respective FUEL PRESS warning light.

Fuel System Schematic
Fuel System Flow: Main/Auxiliary Tanks → Firewall Shutoff Valve → Engine-Driven Boost Pump → Firewall Fuel Filter → Fuel Heater → High-Pressure Fuel Pump → Fuel Control Unit
Additional Considerations
Aviation Gasoline Usage

When using aviation gasoline in climbs above 20,000 feet, the first indication of insufficient fuel pressure may be an intermittent flicker of the red FUEL PRESS switchlights, accompanied by fluctuations in fuel flow and torque. These conditions are eliminated by activating the standby pumps.

Operational Limitation
OPERATIONAL LIMITATION: Operation of either engine with its corresponding fuel pressure warning annunciator illuminated is limited to 10 hours between overhaul or replacement of the high-pressure main engine fuel pump. Windmilling time is not charged against this time limit.
System Interactions
Auxiliary Fuel Transfer

The fuel pressure switch also sends a signal to the auxiliary fuel transfer printed circuit board. If fuel pressure is not available, the NO TRANSFER annunciator will illuminate, indicating auxiliary tank fuel cannot be transferred.

Crossfeed Operation

During crossfeed operations (single-engine only), the standby pump on the delivery side is automatically activated. If a standby pump becomes inoperative, crossfeed can only be accomplished from the side with the operative standby pump.
