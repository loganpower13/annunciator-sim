L CHIP DETECT Warning

Understanding and responding to L CHIP DETECT warnings in the King Air B200

Condition
Ferrous metal particles detected in the left engine oil system
Trigger Point
Magnetic chip detector attracts ferrous metal particles in the oil

BB-1439, 1444 and subsequent: Amber caution light

Prior to BB-1444, except 1439: Red warning light

Chip Detection System Components
Magnetic Chip Detector

Located at the bottom of each reduction gearbox. Contains magnets that attract and capture ferrous (iron-based) metal particles circulating in the engine oil.

When sufficient metal accumulates on the detector, it completes an electrical circuit that illuminates the respective CHIP DETECT annunciator.

Reduction Gearbox Location

The reduction gear section is located at the front of the engine and is a two-stage, planetary type. Its primary function is to reduce the high rpm of the free turbine to the value required for propeller operation.

NOTE: The reduction gear section is also used for torquemeter operation and includes drive sections for the propeller governor, propeller overspeed governor, and propeller tachgenerator.
Oil System Flow

The engine lubrication system is a completely self-contained dry-sump system. Oil is scavenged from the reduction gearcase and returned to the tank through the oil cooler.

Any metal particles from gear wear or bearing deterioration will be carried by the oil flow to the chip detector located at the lowest point of the reduction gearbox.

Response Procedures
Immediate Actions
Monitor Engine Instruments
TORQUE, ITT, OIL PRESS/TEMP, N₁, N₂
Listen for Abnormal Noises
GEAR WHINE, BEARING NOISE
Check for Vibration
UNUSUAL AIRFRAME VIBRATION
Abnormal Indications Requiring Shutdown
SHUTDOWN ENGINE IF ANY OF THE FOLLOWING OCCUR:
  • • Decreasing oil pressure or increasing oil temperature
  • • Fluctuating or decreasing torque
  • • Rising ITT at constant power setting
  • • Unusual mechanical noises or vibration
If No Abnormal Indications
CONTINUED FLIGHT CONSIDERATIONS:

If engine parameters remain normal and no abnormal indications are present, continued flight to destination may be considered. However, the presence of metal particles indicates ongoing mechanical wear that could progress rapidly.

Recommendation: Land at nearest suitable airport for inspection.

System Operation Details
Oil System Operation

When the engine is running, the oil pressure pump draws oil from the tank and directs pressure oil through various filters to the engine bearings, accessory and reduction drive gears, propeller governor, and engine torquemeter system.

All oil is scavenged to the accessory gearcase except the reduction gearcase oil, which goes directly to the oil cooler. A screened scavenge pump returns the gearcase oil to the tank through the oil-fuel heater.

Reduction Gearbox Importance

The reduction gear section reduces the high rpm of the free turbine (approximately 38,000 rpm) to the value required for propeller operation (1,600-2,000 rpm). This gearbox contains critical components including:

  • • Planetary reduction gears
  • • Torquemeter system components
  • • Propeller governor drive
  • • Propeller overspeed governor drive
  • • Propeller tachgenerator drive
Maintenance Considerations
Post-Flight Inspection

After a CHIP DETECT warning, maintenance must:

  • • Remove and inspect the magnetic chip detector
  • • Analyze the type and quantity of metal particles
  • • Perform oil analysis for metal content
  • • Inspect oil filter for additional contamination
  • • Conduct borescope inspection of gearbox if indicated
Common Sources of Metal Particles
  • • Normal gear wear (minimal fine particles)
  • • Bearing deterioration (fine steel particles)
  • • Gear tooth damage (chunks or slivers)
  • • Bearing cage failure (brass or bronze particles)
  • • Foreign object damage
