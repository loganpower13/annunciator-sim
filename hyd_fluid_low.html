HYD FLUID LOW Annunciator

Hydraulic Fluid Level Warning - Landing Gear System Alert

Caution Description
Annunciator
HYD FLUID LOW
Condition
Low hydraulic fluid level detected in the hydraulic power pack reservoir
Trigger Point
Fluid level sensor on the reservoir completes a circuit to illuminate the amber HYD FLUID LOW annunciator
Hydraulic System Overview
Hydraulic Power Pack

The hydraulic landing gear system (Serial Nos. BB-1193, BL-73, and subsequent) is actuated by a hydraulic power pack consisting of:

  • 28-VDC motor-driven hydraulic pump
  • Hydraulic reservoir pressurized by engine bleed air
  • Filters
  • Solenoid-operated selector valve
  • Uplock pressure switch
Reservoir System

The power pack reservoir is divided into two sections:

  • One section supplies the electrically-driven hydraulic pump
  • The other section supplies the hand pump

A fill reservoir just inboard of the left nacelle and forward of the main spar features a cap and dipstick assembly for maintaining system fluid level.

Fluid Type

The system is serviced with MIL-H-5606 hydraulic fluid.

System Testing
Annunciator Test

The HYD FLUID LOW annunciator can be tested by pressing the HYD FLUID SENSOR TEST button on the pilot's subpanel.

Warnings and Notes
Important Warnings
WARNING: After an emergency landing gear extension has been made, do not stow the extension handle or move any landing gear controls or reset any switches or circuit breakers until the airplane is on jacks. These precautions are necessary because the failure may have been in the gear-up circuit, in which case, the gear might retract on the ground. The gear cannot be retracted manually.
NOTE: The landing gear cannot be damaged by continued operation of the hand pump.
Related Systems
Landing Gear Operation

The landing gear is extended and retracted by the power pack in conjunction with three hydraulic actuators, one for each gear.

Normal extension time: Approximately 6 seconds

Pressure Cycling

When the gear is fully retracted:

  • Pressure increases to 2,775 psi, opening the uplock pressure switch
  • This breaks the circuit to the pump motor relay
  • When system leakage drops pressure to 2,475 psi, the switch closes to reestablish power
  • Automatic cycling maintains pressure to keep the gear up and locked
